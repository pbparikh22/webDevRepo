Course Adjustmnets

Hello! This will be the last blog for CS347. In this blog, we will discuss improvements that could help future students who take this course. The way this class was structured, I would not change much. However, I do have one idea for adjusting this class that could help other students. First of all, I would change the course so that we could spend more time on learning about express, Node.js, and Redux. I believe these topics are very important in web development and by spending some time by actually learning and applying these things to what we do could better our understanding of them. Not to mention, it could also help with the projects we create as well. After we finished learning about static websites, we immediately jumped into learning about React and Node.js. At first, everythng seemed fine because we were learning about the fundamentals and were also able to keep up with the videos provided by the professor. However, once we started creating our own apps for the labs, things seemed to go sideways (at least for me) because I did not fully understand how it all worked. Which is why, if we spent a little bit more time applying our knowledge and skills to these topics and not only rely on the labs, I believe we could better equip ourselves for the projects and even create own apps without the assistance of the professor. I would like to thank the twodee website for helping me come up with ideas on how to adjust the course.


Value and Impact

When I came into this course, I had a limited understanding of HTML, CSS and Javascript. As the course progressed, I learned so many new topics that I have never known about. In order to keep up with the new material, the videos the professor posted every week greatly helped my understanding on these new topics, especially on react. The professor clearly stated what he was doing in the videos and showed his execution of the code. Since I learn better when I see the execution of something, the videos helped a lot in my learning. The code below was part of the very first react app we made in class by following what the professor was doing in his video. This code represents a constant variable named addColor located in the App function we created when we first started learning about react. Another way I learned the material was through executing the labs during class time. These labs were challenging, but they also reinforced the concepts related to what we were learning about. They also provided a way for students to collaborate with one another and think critically about the solutions. Since most of us were new to many topics discussed, we all strggled a bit on coming up with solutions. However, these labs allowed us to think beyond what we reviewed in the videos and actually think critically for new answers. As a result, by paying attention to the videos and participating in the labs, I believe other students will be able to succeed in this class. I would like to thank the twodee website for helping me come up with ideas what material helped through this course.
